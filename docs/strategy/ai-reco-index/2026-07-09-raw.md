# AI Recommendation Index — raw outputs — 2026-07-09 snapshot

Methodology: 10 fixed buyer questions, run once each (n=1) through two engines.
Claude column via local `claude` CLI, `--model sonnet`, prompt suffix
"Answer with a ranked list of up to 5 specific products and one line why each."
Run date: 2026-07-09. Google AI Overview column via DataForSEO SERP API
(`/v3/serp/google/organic/live/advanced`), same date — see Section 2 below for
whether it shipped.

Answers vary run to run. This is a snapshot, not a benchmark.

---

## 1. Claude (sonnet) — verbatim outputs

### Q1: "best CRM for a small plumbing business"

1. Jobber — purpose-built for field service/plumbing, with scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and client hub baked in.
2. Housecall Pro — strong scheduling + dispatch + payments combo, popular with solo/small plumbing crews.
3. ServiceTitan — the industry heavyweight for plumbing/HVAC, but pricier and more geared to larger operations.
4. HubSpot CRM (free tier) — good if you mainly need lead tracking/pipeline and don't need field-service-specific dispatch/scheduling.
5. GoHighLevel — flexible all-in-one (CRM + booking + SMS/email automation) if you want to combine marketing/lead-gen with basic CRM in one tool.

### Q2: "best CRM for a cleaning business"

1. Jobber — purpose-built for home-service/cleaning businesses (scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, client hub) with the deepest cleaning-specific workflow templates.
2. Housecall Pro — similar field-service focus to Jobber but stronger built-in payments/financing and a more polished mobile app for crews.
3. ServiceTitan — the enterprise-grade option; overkill for a small crew but strong if you're scaling into a multi-crew, multi-location cleaning franchise.
4. HubSpot CRM (Free/Starter) — best if you want general-purpose pipeline/email tracking and don't need scheduling/dispatch built in.
5. GoHighLevel — best if you want CRM + funnels + automated follow-up/review-requests bundled with marketing, at the cost of a steeper setup curve than the cleaning-specific tools above.

### Q3: "best appointment booking software for a small service business"

1. Calendly — the default for a reason: dead-simple booking links, works standalone or embedded, cheap and reliable for a solo operator or small team.
2. Square Appointments — best if you already take payments/POS with Square; booking, deposits, and payment collection live in one place.
3. Acuity Scheduling — more configurable intake forms, packages, and class/group scheduling than Calendly, good for service businesses with varied offerings (salons, coaches, consultants).
4. Housecall Pro — built for field service (HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, landscaping) — booking plus dispatch, invoicing, and route awareness.
5. Setmore — solid free tier for very small teams that just need booking + reminders without much else.

### Q4: "best AI receptionist for a small business"

1. Smith.ai — Live human-backed AI receptionist with strong call-handling nuance; best if you want a hybrid of AI speed and human judgment for edge cases.
2. Air AI / Air (Air.io) — Fully autonomous voice AI that can handle scheduling and follow-up calls end-to-end; good for high call-volume SMBs wanting minimal human oversight.
3. Dialpad Ai Voice / Dialpad AI Receptionist — Integrates tightly with existing business phone systems, good if you already use Dialpad for calls.
4. Goodcall — Purpose-built AI phone agent for small businesses, easy no-code setup, strong for local service businesses (salons, clinics, restaurants).
5. Ruby (with AI features) / Rosie AI Receptionist — Established virtual receptionist brand with AI answering tiers, good if you want a trusted, long-running provider with a lighter AI tier.

### Q5: "best GoHighLevel alternative"

1. HubSpot — most complete all-in-one CRM/marketing/sales suite with the deepest ecosystem and integrations, though pricier at scale.
2. ActiveCampaign — best-in-class marketing automation and email deliverability for agencies that prioritize campaigns over a full CRM/site-builder stack.
3. Vendasta — closest direct competitor for white-label agencies, purpose-built to resell to local business clients under your own brand.
4. Keap — strongest for small-business owner-operators who want simple CRM + automation + invoicing without GHL's complexity.
5. ClickFunnels — best if the core need is funnel/landing-page conversion rather than full CRM/pipeline management.

### Q6: "best HubSpot alternative for a small business"

1. Zoho CRM — Deepest feature set for the price (sales, marketing, and service in one suite) at a fraction of HubSpot's cost.
2. Pipedrive — Simplest, most sales-rep-friendly pipeline UI; fastest to onboard a small team.
3. Close — Built specifically for small teams doing high-volume outbound calling/email, with built-in dialer and email sequencing.
4. ActiveCampaign — Strongest marketing-automation engine per dollar if lead nurturing matters more than a heavy sales pipeline.
5. Freshsales (Freshworks) — Clean, modern CRM with solid built-in phone/email and AI lead scoring at a low entry price.

### Q7: "best free CRM for a one-person business"

1. HubSpot Free CRM — most generous free tier (unlimited users/contacts), plus free email marketing and forms baked in.
2. Zoho CRM Free — solid free tier for up to 3 users with decent automation and mobile app, good if you might add teammates later.
3. Bitrix24 Free — free tier bundles CRM with tasks, chat, and even a website builder, useful if you want one tool for everything.
4. Freshsales (Freshworks CRM) Free — clean, sales-pipeline-focused UI with built-in phone/email, good for a solo salesperson.
5. Capsule CRM Free — simplest, least bloated option (2 users, 250 contacts) if you just want contacts + pipeline without a kitchen-sink platform.

### Q8: "best missed-call text-back software"

1. Podium — the category leader; missed-call text-back was core to its original pitch, and it's bundled with reviews/payments messaging that most local-service businesses already use.
2. GoHighLevel (LeadConnector) — cheapest way to get it since it's a native workflow trigger inside a full CRM/automation platform, and it's the default choice for agencies white-labeling this exact feature.
3. Weave — strong alternative to Podium, especially entrenched in dental/medical/vet practices where it's bundled with phone system + patient communication.
4. Birdeye — best for multi-location or franchise businesses that also want the reputation-management and review-generation features layered on top of the text-back automation.
5. OpenPhone — best lightweight option for solopreneurs/small teams who just want a modern business phone number with auto-text-back, without a full CRM attached.

### Q9: "best all-in-one platform for a marketing agency serving local businesses"

1. GoHighLevel (GHL) — the category leader: whitelabel CRM, funnels, SMS/email automation, and a snapshot marketplace built specifically for agencies to resell to local-business clients.
2. Vendasta — similar all-in-one core, but leans harder into a fulfillment marketplace so agencies can resell services (SEO, social, listings) they don't produce themselves.
3. Thryv — built directly for the local business (not just the agency), bundling CRM, payments, and scheduling; agencies manage it on the client's behalf rather than white-labeling it as their own.
4. Podium — narrower but excellent at the reviews/reputation + lead-messaging piece that local businesses care about most; often paired with a broader platform rather than replacing one.
5. Birdeye — enterprise-leaning alternative to Podium (reviews, messaging, social, listings), best fit for agencies managing multi-location local business clients.

### Q10: "best voice AI for answering business calls"

1. Bland AI — developer-first voice API with the lowest-latency, most reliable telephony infra for building custom call-answering agents at scale.
2. Synthflow — best no-code option; fastest to stand up a receptionist agent with calendar/CRM integrations without writing code.
3. Air AI (Aidbase/Air) — strong end-to-end conversational reasoning, handles longer multi-turn calls (bookings, qualifying) more naturally than most competitors.
4. Retell AI — most flexible for developers wanting to pair a custom LLM + voice stack (e.g., GPT-4o/Realtime) with solid interruption handling and call analytics.
5. Goodcall — purpose-built for SMBs (restaurants, local services) answering everyday calls (hours, FAQs, bookings) with the least setup friction and a phone-first UI non-technical owners actually use.

SeldonFrame did not appear in any of the 10 Claude answers. That absence is
reported honestly on the page — it is not nudged or asterisked away.

## 2. Google AI Overviews (DataForSEO) — status: NOT SHIPPED in v1

Attempted 2026-07-09 against `POST /v3/serp/google/organic/live/advanced`
(the DataForSEO SERP endpoint that surfaces an `ai_overview` item when Google
renders one), auth via `DATAFORSEO_AUTH_B64` from `packages/crm/.env.local`.

- Three isolated smoke calls for "best CRM for a small plumbing business"
  succeeded (status 20000, cost $0.002–$0.00415 each) and confirmed the
  account has live SERP access. `item_types` on the result advertised
  `ai_overview` as a possible block type, but the `items` array never
  actually contained one across depth-10 and depth-20 requests, with and
  without `load_async_ai_overview: true` — Google did not render an AI
  Overview for this query at request time. A dedicated
  `/v3/serp/google/ai_overview/live/advanced` endpoint does not exist on
  this account (40402 Invalid Path).
- Running the full 10-question batch hit persistent `fetch failed` /
  socket-reset errors from this environment toward the DataForSEO host
  (5 of 10 requests failed outright after 5 retries with exponential
  backoff; the other 5 returned parseable-but-empty responses with `$0`
  cost, i.e. not real SERP data). Total real spend across the whole
  exercise: **$0.00615** (three smoke calls only).
- Per the v1 spec's explicit instruction — never fabricate the column —
  **v1 ships Claude-only.** The Google AI Overviews column is left for a
  follow-up run once connectivity is verified stable, and/or once a query
  set that reliably triggers an AI Overview block is confirmed. The page
  and methodology state this plainly: "v1 measures one engine (Claude);
  Google AI Overviews is next."
