**[WHAT YOU'RE ACTUALLY BUYING]**

Before the list — what you're really trying to buy here is a good-looking site up fast,
without hiring a developer. Keep that in mind, because "best" depends on what happens
*after* someone lands on the page. A website builder gets you a page people can visit.
Whether that visit turns into a booked job is a different question — we'll come back to
that at the end.

**[CHAPTER 1 — Wix]**

Number one, and the most-used drag-and-drop builder out there: Wix. Pricing starts from
about 17 dollars a month. It's got a massive library of templates and add-on apps, full
freedom to drag and drop your own design, and there's even an AI option — Wix ADI — that
builds you a starter site automatically.

[B-ROLL: Wix pricing page]

Here's the honest catch: drag-and-drop freedom means the design is entirely on you. A
badly put-together Wix site looks like a badly put-together Wix site. If you're not
design-minded, that freedom can work against you.

**[CHAPTER 2 — Squarespace]**

Number two: Squarespace, running from about 16 to 25 dollars a month. This is the builder
known for clean, good-looking templates out of the box — great if you want something
beautiful fast without a lot of fiddling. It's also got solid built-in blog and online
store tools, and because Squarespace owns Acuity, booking connects easily.

Watch out for this: you can't customize much beyond the template system, and there's no
CRM or lead-capture built into the site itself. It's a beautiful page — what happens after
someone visits is still up to you.

**[CHAPTER 3 — Durable]**

Number three: Durable, an AI website builder that generates a usable site from a short
prompt in under a minute. The AI-generated draft is free; paid plans start around 12
dollars a month. It's genuinely fast, the free plan is actually usable and not just a demo,
and it comes with a light CRM and invoicing built in.

The catch: it has no AI phone answering at all. "AI" here just means a chat box and
written content — and because so many sites get generated the same way, they can end up
looking similar to each other.

**[CHAPTER 4 — WordPress.com]**

Number four: WordPress dot com — the world's most-used website platform, either hosted
for you or run yourself, from about 4 to 25 dollars a month if you self-host through
WordPress.org, costs vary by your hosting provider. It's got the largest plugin and theme
library out there, full ownership if you self-host, and some of the best tools around for
blog and content SEO.

Here's the tradeoff: hosting WordPress yourself means you — or a developer — are in
charge of security, updates, and hosting. That's real, ongoing work, not a set-it-and-
forget-it choice.

**[CHAPTER 5 — GoDaddy Websites + Marketing]**

Number five: GoDaddy's Websites and Marketing builder, often sold bundled with your
domain name, from about 10 dollars a month. It's the cheapest starting price on this
list, domain, site and email all come bundled together, and it's simple enough for a
first-timer to get going.

The honest catch: design freedom and features are the most limited option here. It's
built for simplicity, not for growing into something bigger later.

**[WHAT ABOUT FREE BUILDERS]**

Quick honest note on free website builders — they exist, and they work fine for one
simple page. But free plans usually make you pay to use your own web address, strip your
SEO controls, and stick their own branding at the bottom of your site. None of them
answer the phone or book the job for you — the site is the whole product, and everything
that happens after a visitor lands is still on you.
